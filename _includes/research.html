Multi-Trophic Extension of Community Assembly

In ecosystems, species are gathering to form complex assemblages (community assembly). To what extent local and regional processes co-operate in community assembly is one of the core questions in ecology. My research motivation is to expand the current community assembly theories to multi-trophic communities.
(ref. Shinohara and Yoshida 2021, Shinohara and Yoshida in press)

Human Impacts on Plant-Insect Interaction Networks

As one of the greatest threads to global biodiversity, land-use changes have unappreciated effect on communities by altering the ways species are interacting (the structures of interaction networks). I'm exploring the mechanisms and consequences of human land-use changes on interaction networks in agricultural landscapes.
(ref. Shinohara et al. 2019)
